Caramel Depositor & Wrapability Analysis¶

Project Objective & Deliverables¶

Identify scrap redound by utilizing depositing 6.25 grams of caramel.
Justify decreasing deposition volume to reduce turtle size variability.
Reduce turtle size variability to increase yield.

Defining Constraints¶

Controlled for caramel moisture at 11.325 percent, which is exceeds the lower specification threshold.

Key Take Aways¶

  1. Caramel moisture was fixed during this experiment at 11.325 percent, which is low relative to our specification. However, caramel viscosity was unobserved, it is reasonable to assume that the caramel viscosity was unpreferable. Additionally, we do not, currently, understand the relationship between moisture and viscosity; therefore, our caramel moisture can be within specification, but viscosity may be unfavorable.
  2. Statistically, the additional caramel did increase turtle volume.
  3. Note, turtle width was statistically unchange, but the t-statistic rides the zenith of decision.
  4. First, we must understand the ways viscosity impacts caramel, then pursue increasing the caramel component. The results above exhibit the product does increase in size, statistically; however, if the scenario is predicatable and controlled, the results could be achieved.
