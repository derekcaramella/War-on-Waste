Wrapability Analysis¶

Project Objective & Deliverables¶

Identify the zenith threshold for length and width setpoints.

Defining Constraints¶

  1. Original Turtles
    1. Former Width: 47 mm
    2. 3 pc. Wrapper Length (Knife Radius): 144 mm
    3. 4 pc. Wrapper Length (Knife Radius): 192
    4. Maximum Turtle Width (Equal Distributed): 48 mm
  2. Bite Size Turtles
    1. Former Width: 46 mm
    2. 3 pc. Wrapper Length (Knife Radius): Unkown
    3. 4 pc. Wrapper Length (Knife Radius): 152 mm
    4. Maximum Turtle Width (Equal Distributed): 38 mm

Key Take Aways at Sigs¶

Either our length setting is too strict on Original or too lenient on Bite.

  1. We are losing roughly 1.9039% of our Original product due to length.
  2. We are losing roughly 0.0009% of our Original product due to width.
  3. We are losing roughly 0.0% of our Original product due to height.
  4. We are losing roughly 1.0934% of our Bite product due to length.
  5. We are losing roughly 0.4104% of our Bite product due to width.
  6. We are losing roughly 0.0198% of our Bite product due to height
  7. For all product formats, to set maximum turtle width, minus the setpoint from 78.
  8. For all product formats, to set maximum turtle height, add the setpoint and 2.

The probability of wrapping 2 or more turtles, in a 3 pc. bar, that are greater than 48 mm is: 0.107%.

We should assume the risk and open the length sensor. If we leave the length sensor, there is 0% chance that a turtle will pass the 3D profile station. When rework occurs, the turtle will be reject again. Applicably, we have roughly 2.0% of Orginial turtles greater than 48 mm; thus, we will see roughly 2.0% of our turtles rejected after rework. The chances of two or more turtles that are greater than 48 mm is roughly 0.107%; therefore, we should loosen our length sensor to allow ou 48 mm turtles through.¶
